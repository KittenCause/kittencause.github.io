Welcome to Kitten Cause

We are an organization raising funds for the Adair County Humane Society so that they may administer FIP tests to the cats they care for.

Kitten Cause was founded by Katie Rericha and Ben Butzow after losing a kitten, Alexander, to FIP. Two of Alexander's three siblings have been tested for FIP and both carry the virus.

What is FIP?

Feline infectious peritonitis (FIP) is a virus that in most cats, does not cause any issues. According to the Cornell Feline Health Center, about five to ten percent of cats develop feline infectious peritonitis virus (FIPV)1. FIPV is ultimately fatal and there is currently no treatment or cure. This disease can be spread to other cats very easily, especially in a shelter when all of the cats are so close to each other due to space limitations.

How can I help?

There are many ways to help. Donations to Kitten Cause or the Adair County Humane Society will help pay for the costs of FIP tests, as well as the possible construction of a new shelter for FIP positive cats.

You can also purchase Kitten Cause stickers and shirts from our shop. A portion of all sales will go to the Adair County Humane Society.

Help spread the word by liking our Facebook page and telling your friends about Kitten Cause.
